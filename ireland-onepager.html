Why Now — Three Irreversible Catalysts

March 2026
Semaglutide Patent Cliff

Primary patent expired globally. India, Canada, Brazil, China (40% of world population) gain generic access. EU follows shortly thereafter. Global GLP-1 user base projected to grow 2–3× — and every new user is a potential Monch customer.

2026 — Ongoing
Oral GLP-1 Pills Launch

Oral formulations remove the injection barrier. Clinical data shows ~80% GI side-effect rates with oral delivery — even higher than injectables. This expands the addressable patient pool dramatically.

Q1 2026
Gut Health = Capital Magnet

Gut-health products captured 30% of food venture capital in Q1 2026. The science of microbiome-mediated GLP-1 modulation — Monch's mechanism — is the most-funded sub-thesis in functional nutrition.

The Dual Revenue Engine — Why a European Factory, Not Just a Brand

Engine 1 · Branded Consumer Products

Monch Protein + Fiber Bar (Y1 flagship), Monch Electrolyte Packet (Y1 attach companion), Monch Functional Drink (H1 Y2). Built with the team behind Muscle Milk. Base-case Y2 revenue: $3.6M with 57% gross margin. Strong, but on its own this consumes only ~43% of current production capacity.

The factory case is built on Engine 2.

Engine 2 · B2B Ingredient Licensing — The Scale Play

Water-soluble Monch can be incorporated into any consumer food product — bars, drinks, baked goods, dairy. At a target production cost of $0.10/serving (vs. $0.37 today), Monch becomes credible to license to Cargill, Tate & Lyle, ADM, IFF, and DSM-Firmenich at supplier price.

Typical co-development deal shape: 500 MT/yr minimum, 5–7 year category exclusivity. This is the channel that requires — and justifies — plant-scale European manufacturing.

Why Ireland — Four Strategic Pillars

Science Cluster Alignment

APC Microbiome Ireland (UCC, Cork)

Monch's mechanism — water-soluble fiber → SCFA fermentation → microbiome modulation → endogenous GLP-1 production — sits squarely in APC's research domain. APC is one of the world's three most-cited microbiome centres. A formal R&D partnership accelerates clinical validation and underpins KDB-qualifying IP development on Irish soil.

Pharma Ecosystem Adjacency

Companion to a Cluster That Already Exists

Lilly (manufacturer of tirzepatide / Mounjaro / Zepbound), Pfizer, AbbVie, MSD, and Novo Nordisk-adjacent partners all manufacture in Ireland. A GLP-1 companion nutrition platform is the natural civilian-side bookend to that existing pharmaceutical-grade infrastructure — supply chain, regulatory, and talent pools transfer.

Market Access

EU Production Base, Post-Patent-Cliff

The semaglutide patent cliff opens EU markets to generic GLP-1 drugs and a massive new patient cohort needing companion products. An Irish facility serves the EU, UK, Middle East, and Africa from inside the single market — and dual-sources us against current China-sole supply concentration.

Fiscal & Capital Fit

KDB + R&D Credit + IDA Capital Grants

The Knowledge Development Box (6.25%) on qualifying IP-derived income is purpose-built for the Engine-2 ingredient-licensing royalty stream. Combined with the 25% R&D Tax Credit, 12.5% corporation tax, and IDA capital/training grants, Ireland is the most fiscally efficient EU jurisdiction for an IP-heavy ingredient business.
